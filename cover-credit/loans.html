⚠ What Banks Won't Tell You

Four things your lender
hopes you never find out.

Most borrowers discover these only after signing. We tell you upfront — even if it costs us a commission.

01
🎭
"Flat Rate 12%" is actually ~22% in real terms
Banks advertise a flat interest rate — calculated on the original loan amount throughout the tenure. But you're paying back the principal every month. The actual effective annual rate (reducing balance) is nearly double the flat rate quoted.
Ask: "Is this flat rate or reducing balance rate?" — most agents won't say unless pushed.
02
📦
"Zero Processing Fee" still has ₹30,000–₹80,000 in charges
Waived fee is the bait. Hidden costs: loan insurance premium added to principal, legal & technical charges, MODT stamp duty, CERSAI, franking, and GST on all fees. On ₹50L, true upfront cost is often ₹75,000–₹1.2L.
Always ask for a complete "schedule of charges" — not just the sanction letter.
03
🔒
Pre-payment penalties buried in Page 18 cost ₹50,000+
Got a bonus? Planning to prepay? Private banks and NBFCs charge 2–3% on the prepaid amount. Prepay ₹10L mid-tenure = ₹20,000–₹30,000 gone instantly. We check every prepayment clause before you sign.
Floating-rate home loans from scheduled banks: RBI mandates zero prepayment charges.
04
🏛️
Your salary account bank almost never gives the best rate
Your bank has zero incentive to price-compete — you're already their customer. We've seen salary-account holders get 9.8% from their own bank and 8.4% from a competitor. That 1.4% gap on ₹50L over 20 years = ₹7.8 lakh extra.
₹7,80,000 saved last year — just by comparing instead of walking into their own bank.

Real Questions We Get

Questions nobody
else answers honestly.

Can I negotiate my interest rate — and how?+

Yes, and most people don't try. If your CIBIL is 780+, you have significant power. Tell them you have competing offers from two other lenders. Banks regularly reduce rates 0.25–0.5% for customers who negotiate. On ₹30L over 15 years, 0.5% = ₹1.8 lakh saved.

Flat rate vs reducing balance — explained simply.+

Flat: interest on the full original amount throughout. Reducing: interest on what you still owe — decreasing monthly. Flat 12% = ~21.5% reducing balance. Always confirm which method before comparing rates.

Should I foreclose my home loan early or invest the money?+

Depends on your rate vs investment return after tax. If your loan is 8.5% and you're in 30% bracket, effective cost with Sec 24B = ~6.9%. Equity mutual funds have historically returned 12%+ over 10+ years. Mathematically, investing often beats prepaying. We run this calculation free.

My loan application was rejected. What now?+

Don't apply anywhere else immediately — each rejection leaves a hard enquiry that drops your score further. Get the rejection reason in writing. We analyse it and give you a specific 60–90 day recovery plan — then approach the right lender for your exact situation.

Is there a way to get a home loan without income proof?+

Some NBFCs offer Bank Statement Assessment Loans — evaluating 12–24 months of bank statements instead of ITR. These carry 1–2% higher rates but are legitimate. We have relationships with 6 such lenders in AP and Telangana.

How long does the whole process take?+

Consultation to comparison: 48 hours. Sanction: 7–15 working days for most lenders. Disbursal: 3–7 days after sanction. Total: typically 3–5 weeks. We've done it in 11 working days for ready-to-occupy properties with clean documents.
