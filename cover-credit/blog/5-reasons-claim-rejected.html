1. Non-Disclosure of Pre-Existing Conditions

When you buy a policy, you're required to disclose all pre-existing medical conditions like diabetes, hypertension, thyroid, or heart disease. Many people either forget or deliberately hide these, hoping it won't matter. It will. Insurers check your medical history during claims, and any mismatch can lead to rejection ‚Äî or even policy cancellation.

What to do: Always disclose all conditions honestly. Policies still cover pre-existing conditions ‚Äî just after a waiting period (usually 2‚Äì4 years).

Health insurance excludes cosmetic procedures, weight loss surgeries, infertility treatments (in most plans), and experimental therapies. Claims for these will be rejected outright, regardless of how the admission was coded.
