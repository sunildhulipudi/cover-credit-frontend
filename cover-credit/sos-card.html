Common questions

Everything you need to know

What should I do immediately after a vehicle accident in India? ▾
Call your insurer's claims helpline within the first hour — most insurers require registration within 24 hours. Before anything else, photograph all damage and road positions. Do not move the vehicle until the surveyor arrives or your insurer gives explicit permission. File an FIR at the nearest police station if third-party damage is involved or if damages exceed ₹1 lakh. Never admit fault at the scene.
Why do vehicle insurance claims get rejected in India? ▾
Most rejections are procedural, not policy-related. The most common reasons are: moving the vehicle before the surveyor inspects it, failing to file FIR within the required window, getting repairs done at a non-network garage, not registering the claim within 24 hours of the incident, and not having the right documents at the time of claim. The SOS card prevents all five.
How does cashless health insurance hospitalisation work? ▾
Go directly to a network hospital — call your insurer's helpline to confirm the nearest one before you leave. At the admission desk, identify yourself as a cashless policyholder and show your insurance card plus Aadhaar. The hospital sends a pre-authorisation request to your insurer, which is typically approved in 2–4 hours. You pay nothing upfront for covered expenses. At discharge, only non-covered items (if any) need to be paid by you.
What is IMT-23 and do I need it for my lorry? ▾
IMT-23 is an endorsement that extends commercial vehicle insurance to cover goods carried in the vehicle itself — the body, tarpaulin, ropes, accessories. Without IMT-23, if the lorry body or its equipment is damaged in an accident, the claim for those items will be rejected. Most fleet owners who discover they're missing IMT-23 do so only after a rejection. If you're unsure whether your policy includes it, check your policy schedule or call your advisor.
Can I generate cards for multiple policies? ▾
Yes — select "Multiple policies" mode at the top of the form. Fill each policy individually and click "Add to bundle."
