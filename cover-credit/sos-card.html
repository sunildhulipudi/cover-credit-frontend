Common questions

Everything you need to know

What should I do immediately after a vehicle accident in India? ▾
Call your insurer's claims helpline within the first hour — most insurers require registration within 24 hours. Photograph all damage before moving the vehicle. Do not move the vehicle until the surveyor arrives. File an FIR if third-party damage is involved or if damages exceed ₹1 lakh. Never admit fault at the scene.
Why do vehicle insurance claims get rejected in India? ▾
Most rejections are procedural, not policy-related. Moving the vehicle before the surveyor, failing to file FIR within the required window, getting repairs done at a non-network garage, not registering the claim within 24 hours, or not having the right documents. The SOS card prevents all five.
How does cashless health insurance hospitalisation work? ▾
Go directly to a network hospital — call your insurer's helpline to confirm the nearest one before you leave. At the admission desk, identify yourself as a cashless policyholder and show your insurance card plus Aadhaar. Pre-authorisation is typically approved in 2–4 hours. You pay nothing upfront for covered expenses.
What is IMT-23 and do I need it for my lorry? ▾
IMT-23 is an endorsement that extends commercial vehicle insurance to cover goods carried in the vehicle itself — the body, tarpaulin, ropes, accessories. Without IMT-23, if the lorry body or its equipment is damaged in an accident, the claim for those items will be rejected. Most fleet owners who discover they're missing IMT-23 do so only after a rejection.
