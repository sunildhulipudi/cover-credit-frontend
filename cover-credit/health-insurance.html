Frequently Asked Questions

What is a waiting period in health insurance? +

A waiting period is a time during which certain conditions or diseases are not covered. Most plans have a 30-day waiting period for non-accident claims and 2‚Äì4 years for pre-existing conditions. We help you find plans with shorter waiting periods.

Can I include my parents in a family floater? +

Technically yes, but it's usually not advisable. Since the premium is based on the oldest member's age, adding senior parents inflates the cost significantly. Separate senior citizen plans are often a better and more economical choice.

How does cashless hospitalisation work? +

Go to any hospital in your insurer's network, show your health card, get treatment, and the insurer pays the hospital directly. You only pay for non-covered items. We assist in finding network hospitals near you in AP & Telangana.
