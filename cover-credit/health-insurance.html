Coverage

What is covered — and what is not

Most surprises at claim time happen because nobody explained exclusions at purchase. We walk you through this before you buy.

Typically Covered ✓
  • ✅Inpatient hospitalisation (24hrs+)
  • ✅ICU & critical care charges
  • ✅Pre-hospitalisation (30–60 days)
  • ✅Post-hospitalisation (60–90 days)
  • ✅500+ daycare procedures
  • ✅Ambulance charges
  • ✅Organ donor expenses
  • ✅Mental illness treatment (most new plans)
  • ✅AYUSH treatments
  • ✅Domiciliary hospitalisation
Typically NOT Covered ✗
  • ❌Cosmetic or aesthetic procedures
  • ❌Dental (unless due to accident)
  • ❌Spectacles, hearing aids, contact lenses
  • ❌Pre-existing conditions (during waiting period)
  • ❌OPD consultations (basic plans)
  • ❌Maternity (first 2–4 years in most plans)
  • ❌Weight loss / obesity surgery
  • ❌Experimental treatments
  • ❌Self-inflicted injuries
  • ❌War or nuclear hazard illness

Buyer Traps

6 mistakes that cost
families lakhs of rupees

These are the exact mistakes we prevent when we sell you a policy — so nothing surprises you later.

01
Not Disclosing Pre-Existing Conditions
Hiding diabetes, BP, or past surgeries to get a lower premium. Insurers check medical history during every claim — this gets policies cancelled.
We ensure full honest disclosure so your policy holds up when needed.
02
Buying Cheapest Without Reading Exclusions
Low-premium plans hide ₹1,500 room rent sub-limits that trigger proportional deduction of your entire bill — not just the room charge.
We show you the actual effective coverage — not just the headline premium.
03
Choosing Wrong Sum Insured
Families in Hyderabad buy ₹3L cover and discover that a single surgery costs ₹4–6L. Underinsurance is one of the most common mistakes we see.
We calculate the right cover based on your city, hospital preference, and family size.
04
Putting Parents in a Family Floater
Including elderly parents inflates the premium for the entire family by 40–60% because rates are based on the oldest member's age.
We recommend separate senior plans for parents — same or better cover, lower total cost.
05
Ignoring the Waiting Period
30-day initial wait. 2–4 years for pre-existing diseases. 1–2 years for specific surgeries like cataract, hernia. Buying late means waiting longer.
We tell you the exact waiting period for every condition before you buy.
06
Letting the Policy Lapse at Renewal
Even a 1-day lapse resets your waiting period, cancels no-claim bonus, and makes pre-existing conditions ineligible again from scratch.
We send you renewal reminders 30, 15, and 5 days before expiry — free of charge.

FAQ

Questions we get
every single day

What is the ideal sum insured for a family of 4 in Hyderabad?+

Minimum ₹10 lakh for a family of 4 with parents below 40. Given hospital costs at Apollo, Care, and Yashoda in Hyderabad, ₹15–20 lakh with a ₹20–50 lakh super top-up is ideal. We calculate the exact right amount for your family — contact us for a personalised recommendation.

Should I include my parents in a family floater or buy a separate plan?+

Almost always separate. When parents are added to a floater, the premium is calculated on the oldest member's age — inflating your cost by 40–60%. A dedicated senior citizen plan for parents aged 55+ gives the same or better coverage at a significantly lower total premium.

What is a waiting period — and why does it matter?+

Every policy has waiting periods: (1) 30-day initial wait for non-accident claims, (2) 2–4 years for pre-existing conditions like diabetes or hypertension, (3) 1–2 years for specific conditions like cataract, hernia, joint replacement. We explain every waiting period clearly before you sign — so nothing surprises you.

My employer gives ₹5L group cover. Do I still need personal insurance?+

Yes. Group cover ends the day you leave your job. ₹5L is insufficient for major surgeries in Hyderabad. A personal ₹10L floater + ₹30L super top-up costs ₹8,000–12,000 per year and gives you permanent, job-independent protection you control.

Does health insurance cover pre-existing conditions like diabetes?+

Yes — after the waiting period (usually 2–4 years). The key is to disclose all conditions honestly at purchase. If you declare diabetes, the insurer may load your premium slightly but will cover related hospitalisation after waiting. Non-disclosure at purchase is the #1 reason policies get cancelled.

Do you help with renewals?+

Yes — renewal reminders are a free service we provide to every customer we sell a policy to. We send reminders 30 days, 15 days, and 5 days before expiry so you never miss a renewal and never lose your continuity benefits.
